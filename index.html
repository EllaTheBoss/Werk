Werk is an app for students on college campuses that facilitates academic collaboration and social networking by creating a graphing database that connects people based on school, major, common interests, classes taken, and current enrollment.

Werk helps students build their future professional and social networks at an early stage.

It enables students to find on-the-spot study groups at any time of the day or night.

And it connects students to peers who've hav valuable experiences and are willing to share insight in exchange for coffee, food, new connections, and new friends.

TAs are also incentivized to use the app to connect with students more efficiently.

Study groups and student collaboration are a cultural benefit that is vital to academic success. But many college students can struggle to create that network.

Werk creates an inclusive academic and social culture which allows the cultural benefits of a small academic environment to scale up to fit large universities.

The conglomeration of data on various student body democraphics creates a valuable database that can provide insights to educational institutions, private tutoring services, publishers, and others as a core pillar of Werk's business model.

P-Sets and Chill: While studying, students can enable location visibility on a map. They can see the studying location of other users in their network who’ve enabled location visibility. Perhaps they’re working on the same assignment and would like to compare answers? Day or night, a study group is at your fingertips.

Werk is scaling the innovation frontier in today's economy of trust.

Our application strives to decentralize the flow of academic collaboration and social networking on college campuses.

Fostering horizontal relationships within the student body will limit the central oversight power of institutions to empower individuals.

The developing economy of trust has shown that bypassing central administration is cheaper and more efficient, and broadens the market.

Werk’s algorithms provide a means to bypass the bottlenecked model of the Office of Student Engagement to put networking initiative directly in the hands of students so they can take control of their own education and career networking.
